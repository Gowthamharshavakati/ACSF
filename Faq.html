FAQ
    1. What is shrimp farming?

    A.Shrimp farming, or shrimp aquaculture, refers to the cultivation of shrimp in artificially created ponds or tanks for commercial purposes.

    2.Where is shrimp farming practiced?

    A.Shrimp farming is practiced in coastal regions with suitable environmental conditions, such as Southeast Asia, Latin America, and parts of the United States.

    3. What types of shrimp are farmed?

    A.The most common species farmed include Pacific white shrimp (Litopenaeus vannamei) and black tiger shrimp (Penaeus monodon)..

    4.How is shrimp farming done?

    A.Shrimp are typically raised in ponds or tanks filled with seawater or brackish water. Farmers manage water quality, feed the shrimp, and protect them from diseases.

    5.What are the environmental impacts of shrimp farming?

    A.Shrimp farming can lead to habitat destruction, water pollution, and the depletion of wild fish stocks used for feed. Sustainable practices aim to minimize these impacts.

    6.What are the main challenges in shrimp farming?

    A.Disease outbreaks, managing water quality, securing sustainable feed sources, and market price volatility are significant challenges in shrimp farming.
