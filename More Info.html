TIGER PRAWNS

Tiger prawns (Penaeus monodon) are a species of marine crustaceans that are highly prized for their sweet and tender flesh. Native to the Indo-Pacific region, they are widely farmed and exported around the world. Tiger prawns are characterized by their large size, with some individuals reaching up to 13 inches in length, and their distinctive stripes, which give them their name. They are a versatile ingredient and can be prepared in a variety of ways, including grilling, baking, and stir-frying. Tiger prawns are also an important source of nutrition, providing high amounts of protein, selenium, and vitamin B12. They are also low in fat and calories, making them a popular choice for health-conscious consumers. Whether fresh or frozen, tiger prawns are a delicious and sustainable seafood option that is sure to please even the most discerning palate.

Here are some points about Tiger prawns:
- Scientific name: Penaeus monodon
- Native to: Indo-Pacific region
- Flavor: Sweet and tender
- Texture: Firm and crunchy
- Nutrition: High protein, low fat, good source of selenium and vitamin B12
- Uses: Grilled, baked, stir-fries, curries, salads, skewers, etc.
- Pair well with: Garlic, ginger, lemongrass, chilli, lime, coriander, cumin
- Similar to: King prawns, Vannamei prawns
- Widely farmed and exported
- Popular seafood ingredient globally
- Available fresh or frozen
- Can grow up to 13 inches long
- Females can carry up to 500,000 eggs
- Have a lifespan of 2-3 years in the wild

VANNAMEI PRAWNS

Vannamei prawns, also known as whiteleg shrimp or Pacific white shrimp, are a popular seafood ingredient that can be found in many different dishes. Here are some key things to know about vannamei prawns:
- Vannamei prawns are native to the eastern Pacific Ocean and are commonly caught or farmed for food.
- They have a mild and sweet flavor, which makes them a versatile ingredient in many different cuisines.
- They are a good source of protein and are low in fat. They are also a good source of omega-3 fatty acids, which are beneficial for heart health. Here are some points about Vannamei prawns:
- Scientific name: Litopenaeus vannamei
- Native to: Eastern Pacific Ocean
- Flavor: Mild and sweet
- Texture: Firm and juicy
- Nutrition: High protein, low fat, good source of omega-3 fatty acids
- Uses: Curries, stir-fries, salads, shrimp cocktails, etc.
- Pair well with: Garlic, ginger, chilli, citrus, noodles, rice, salads, dumplings
- Similar to: Tiger prawns, king prawns
- Widely farmed and exported
- Popular seafood ingredient globally
- Available fresh or frozen

SCRAMPI PRAWNS:

Scampi prawns are a culinary delight that originated in Italy and have since become a popular dish worldwide. Also known as langostino or Dublin Bay prawns, scampi prawns are actually a type of lobster, not prawns, and are characterized by their delicate flavor and succulent texture. They are typically cooked in garlic butter, white wine, and lemon juice, which enhances their natural sweetness and adds a rich, savory flavor. Scampi prawns are often served as an appetizer or main course, and are commonly accompanied by rice, pasta, or vegetables. Their tender flesh and flavorful sauce make them a beloved treat for seafood lovers, and their ease of preparation has made them a staple in many restaurants and home kitchens. Whether you're a seasoned foodie or a culinary newcomer, scampi prawns are sure to delight your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Here are some points about Scampi Prawns:
- Also known as Langostino or Dublin Bay Prawns
- Actually a type of lobster, not prawns
- Delicate flavor and succulent texture
- Typically cooked in garlic butter, white wine, and lemon juice
- Often served as an appetizer or main course
- Commonly accompanied by rice, pasta, or vegetables
- Tender flesh and flavorful sauce
- Beloved by seafood lovers
- Easy to prepare
- Staple in many restaurants and home kitchens
- Originated in Italy
- Popular worldwide
